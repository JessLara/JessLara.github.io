About Me

With a focus on symbolistic design, I want to use UX/UI as a means to create user-centered designs. I want to use design to reach a wider audience in different spectrums; politics, the medical field, or students in a classroom. Designing a user interface that can transcend the language barrier is my ultimate goal as a designer. I'm driven to solve problems people face everyday.

Growing up in New York City exposed me to many different cultures, languages, and people. It is difficult for non-English speakers to flourish in a society where they struggle to understand the language around them. A simple task like paying a light bill, can become a task that an immigrant has to wait for a person to help them translate, understand, and ultimately pay. I’ve always been interested in mission based organizations; the idea of creating a tool that can help an individual, families, or organizations has been a driver in deciding to pursue UX/UI Design. I want to create designs that aren’t limited by language.

what introduced you to design?

My mom is a seamstress and when I was young she taught me the basics of crochet. The first chance I had, I went to the library and found crocheting books, full of designs and all with different patterns. I went from teaching myself crochet to knitting, embroidery, watercolor, and many other skills I was driven to learn about. Design is something that I have always been passionate about and I feel lucky to be able to apply this in my future career. I’ve always been fascinated with human interactions; it was the reason I pursued a B.A. in Sociology. UX/UI Design is the perfect crossroads to my passion, it emcompasses designing, learning, and creating with the user always in mind.

why is design important to you?

Immigrating to the New York city at a young age, immersed me into a vastly different multi-cultural diaspora. My mom wasn’t able to understand English in the crotchet design books, but she was able to understand the patterns and designs. We could both work on the same items and not be hindered by the language barrier of the books being in English. The patterns are universal. Patterns and symbolism in design can transcend languages. This is what love about design. Design has the ability to reach everyone. I graduated college with a BA in Sociology because I was passionate about learning how humans interact in society, learning about human patterns, cultures, and how they function in an everyday world. My passion in Sociology aids in my knowledge in UX/UI and creating designs that everyone can use.
